1. Theory & Answer Structures
Abstraction Ladder

Mọi ý kiến đều nằm trên một thang từ cụ thể → trừu tượng. Leo lên nghĩa là khái quát hoá thành ý lớn hơn; xuống thang nghĩa là đưa ví dụ thực tế để minh chứng. Người giao tiếp tốt biết di chuyển linh hoạt giữa hai đầu thang.

↑ Abstract
Values, principles
Big idea — conclusion or belief
"Social media is changing what we think success looks like."
· Middle
Pattern, observation
Observation — a trend or category
"People increasingly measure their own progress by comparing it to curated highlights online."
↓ Concrete
Fact, example
Specific fact — something verifiable
"A friend of mine got promoted last year and said it felt empty because it looked better on Instagram than it felt in real life."
Leo lên ↑ khi nào? Đang sa vào chi tiết — lùi lại, khái quát hoá, và kéo mọi người về cùng bức tranh toàn cảnh.
Xuống thang ↓ khi nào? Ý kiến còn mơ hồ — đưa ví dụ hoặc dẫn chứng cụ thể để thuyết phục và khó bị phản bác hơn.
PREP: Point (Trừu tượng) → Reason (Giữa) → Example (Cụ thể) → Point (Trừu tượng).
SITA: Situation / Issue (Cụ thể) → Take (Giữa / Trừu tượng) → Ask (đồng thuận nhóm).
2. Common Structures

Structure A · P-R-E-P

Dùng cho câu hỏi ý kiến, thảo luận, phản biện lịch sự trong meeting.
P
Point — Quan điểm
"I think the best approach here is..."
R
Reason — Lý do
"Because it helps us reduce..."
E
Example — Ví dụ
"For instance, last month our team..."
P
Point — Tóm lại
"So that's why I'd recommend..."

Structure B · S-I-T-A

Dùng khi update tiến độ, báo cáo vấn đề, đề xuất giải pháp.
S
Situation — Bối cảnh
"Currently, we're at stage 2 of..."
I
Issue — Vấn đề
"The challenge we're facing is..."
T
Take — Đề xuất
"What I'd suggest is we..."
A
Ask — Xác nhận
"Does that work for everyone?"
Enrich your answers with 5W1H
Lồng các câu hỏi What / Why / Where / When / How / Who vào từng bước của PREP hoặc SITA để câu trả lời đầy đủ và thuyết phục hơn.
PREP Point = What do I think?
Reason = Why?
Example = When / How did this happen?
Point = What is my conclusion?
SITA Situation = What / Where / When?
Issue = What went wrong & Why?
Take = How to fix?
Ask = Who agrees?

4. Idea Development — when you run out of things to say
Sau khi nêu quan điểm, dùng các cách dưới để tìm thêm luận điểm hoặc mở rộng câu trả lời.
Look through different lenses
Money
Tiết kiệm / tốn tiền?
Giá trị có xứng chi phí?
"It saves money because..."
Time
Nhanh hơn / tiết kiệm thời gian?
Giảm công sức không?
"It is more efficient because..."
Health
Tốt / xấu cho sức khỏe?
Giảm stress? Cải thiện tâm lý?
"It is better for wellbeing..."
Society
Ảnh hưởng cộng đồng / môi trường?
Cải thiện các mối quan hệ?
"It affects society because..."
Education
Học được gì? Phát triển kỹ năng nào?
Mở rộng nhận thức không?
"You can learn a lot from..."
Career
Cơ hội nghề nghiệp / thăng tiến?
Tăng năng lực cạnh tranh?
"Professionally, it helps you..."
Ways to expand your answer
Ý đối lập
Nêu mặt ngược lại rồi bác bỏ hoặc thừa nhận
Cho thấy bạn nhìn nhận đa chiều
"On the other hand... but..."
Ví dụ cụ thể
Đưa số liệu, sự kiện, câu chuyện thực
Cụ thể hóa luận điểm trừu tượng
"For example, in my experience..."
Cá nhân hóa
Gắn với trải nghiệm bản thân
Làm câu trả lời trở nên thật hơn
"Personally, I have noticed..."
Nguyên nhân sâu
Hỏi: tại sao điều đó xảy ra?
Đào sâu hơn một lớp so với câu trả lời đầu
"The reason behind this is..."
Phạm vi rộng hơn
Mở rộng ra xã hội, thế giới, tương lai
Tạo tầm nhìn vĩ mô
"More broadly, this affects..."
Phản biện trước
Tự đặt câu hỏi phản biện rồi trả lời
Ngăn người nghe phản bác
"Some might argue... however..."
Ví dụ — Q: Should people work remotely?
P: I think remote work is beneficial for most employees.
R (Time): It saves commuting time and increases focus.
R (Health): It reduces stress and improves work-life balance.
R (ý đối lập): Some argue it reduces collaboration — but video calls and async tools largely solve that.
E: For example, I save nearly two hours every day by not commuting.
P: That is why I believe remote work genuinely improves quality of life.

6. B1 Grammar Structures for Speaking
Các cấu trúc ngữ pháp thực dụng ở level B1 — dùng ngay trong speaking mà không cần nghĩ nhiều.
Structure
Example
I think / believe / feel that + S + V
I think that working from home is more productive for most people.
In my opinion, + S + V
In my opinion, social media does more harm than good.
To me, + S + V + because + reason
To me, learning English is essential because it opens more career opportunities.
I'm not sure, but I think + S + V
I'm not sure, but I think remote work will become the standard in the future.
Structure
Example
S + V + because + S + V
I prefer online classes because I can study at my own pace.
The main reason is that + S + V
The main reason is that people don't practise speaking enough outside class.
This is because + S + V
Many people feel burnt out. This is because they don't set clear boundaries.
That's why + S + V
Stress builds up over time — that's why taking breaks is so important.
Structure
Example
S + V + adj-er + than + N
Speaking in a small group is less stressful than presenting to a large audience.
S + V + more/less + adj + than + N
Online communication is more convenient than meeting in person, but less personal.
Compared to N, S + V
Compared to five years ago, people rely much more on technology in their daily lives.
While S + V, S + V
While some people enjoy working in an office, others find it hard to focus there.
Structure
Example
For example / For instance, S + V
Many people feel pressure to succeed early. For example, some feel they need to own a house before thirty.
A good example of this is + N
Social media creates unrealistic expectations. A good example of this is the way people only post their highlight moments online.
This is the + adj + N + that + S + V
This is the kind of skill that you can only develop through consistent practice.
Structure
Example
Not only + adj/V, but also + adj/V
Regular exercise is not only good for your body, but also great for your mental health.
Another point is that + S + V
Working from home saves commuting time. Another point is that it reduces daily expenses.
On top of that, S + V
She manages a full-time job and studies in the evenings. On top of that, she volunteers on weekends.
Structure
Example
Although / Even though + S + V, S + V
Although social media has many benefits, it can seriously affect mental health if overused.
S + V, but + S + V
University is expensive, but the network and experience you gain can be invaluable.
I understand that S + V, but I still think + S + V
I understand that working long hours shows dedication, but I still think rest is equally important.
Structure
Example
If + S + V (present), S + will + V
If you practise speaking every day, you will see improvement within a few weeks.
If + S + V (past), S + would + V
If I had more free time, I would travel more and explore new cultures.
Structure
Example
Overall / In general, S + V
Overall, I believe a healthy work-life balance leads to better long-term performance.
That's why I believe + S + V
Burnout is becoming more common — that's why I believe companies need to take mental health more seriously.
So, to sum up, + S + V
So, to sum up, consistency and practice make the biggest difference in language learning.

Topic A · Work from home vs. Office
Do you prefer working from home or the office? Why?Example ▾
I actually prefer working from the office. From my perspective, being around colleagues helps me stay focused and communicate more efficiently. For instance, quick questions that would take 10 emails back and forth get resolved in 2 minutes face-to-face. So that's why I feel I'm more productive in person.
What's the hardest part about working remotely for you?Example ▾
Honestly, the hardest part is separating work time from personal time. Because my workspace and living space are the same, I find it difficult to truly unwind after hours. I sometimes catch myself checking messages late at night, which affects my sleep.
How do you stay focused when you work from home?Example ▾
I've found that having a consistent morning routine makes a big difference. I get dressed as if I'm going to the office, set a clear start time, and use the Pomodoro technique — 25 minutes of deep work followed by a 5-minute break. It keeps me from doom-scrolling.
Do you think remote work affects teamwork? How?Example ▾
I think it does, both positively and negatively. On one hand, remote work forces teams to document everything better and communicate more intentionally. On the other hand, it can reduce the sense of belonging — small informal interactions that build team morale tend to disappear.
What tools or habits help you communicate better online?Example ▾
The tools I rely on most are Slack for async updates and Google Meet for anything that needs real-time discussion. One habit I've found helpful is always starting messages with context — instead of just "Can we talk?", I write "Quick question about the deployment timeline — do you have 10 minutes today?"
If you could design the perfect work setup, what would it look like?Example ▾
My ideal setup would be a hybrid model — 3 days in the office, 2 days remote. The office days would be for team meetings and collaborative work, while the remote days would be dedicated to deep, focused tasks. I'd also want a quiet dedicated workspace at home, not just a corner of the bedroom.
